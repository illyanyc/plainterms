You've done it thousands of times: scrolled to the bottom of a wall of legal text and clicked "I Agree." But buried in those terms are clauses specifically designed to benefit the company — often at your expense.

Here are five of the most common (and costly) hidden clauses, and what you can do about them.

## 1. Automatic Renewal with Silent Price Increases

Most subscription services auto-renew by default. That's inconvenient but expected. What catches people off guard is the clause that lets the company **raise the price without explicit notice**.

Language like "we reserve the right to adjust pricing at any time" means your $9.99/month subscription could jump to $14.99 with nothing more than an email buried in your promotions folder.

**What to do:** Set calendar reminders before renewal dates. Use PlainTerms to flag auto-renewal clauses instantly.

## 2. Binding Arbitration

This one is a heavyweight. A binding arbitration clause means you **waive your right to sue the company in court**. Instead, disputes go to a private arbitrator — often chosen by the company.

In practice, this means:
- No jury trial
- Limited discovery (the process of gathering evidence)
- Outcomes that statistically favor the corporate party
- No public record of the proceedings

Some companies include a 30-day opt-out window after you accept the terms. Miss that window, and you're locked in.

**What to do:** Look for opt-out instructions immediately. PlainTerms flags binding arbitration as a high-severity red flag and highlights any opt-out windows.

## 3. Class Action Waivers

Often paired with binding arbitration, a class action waiver prevents you from joining a group lawsuit against the company. This is enormously valuable to companies — individual arbitration claims are rarely worth pursuing for small amounts.

Consider this: if a company overcharges 500,000 users by $3 each, a class action suit for $1.5 million is viable. But no individual is going to arbitrate over $3.

**What to do:** Check if your state has laws limiting class action waivers. Some courts have found these unenforceable for small consumer claims.

## 4. Broad Content Licensing

Upload a photo, write a review, or post a comment? Many platforms include clauses granting themselves a **"worldwide, royalty-free, perpetual, irrevocable license"** to your content.

This means they can:
- Use your content in advertising
- Sublicense it to third parties
- Continue using it even after you delete your account

The scope often goes far beyond what's needed for the service to function.

**What to do:** Read the content licensing section before uploading anything valuable. Some platforms offer more limited licenses — PlainTerms will highlight the exact scope.

## 5. Unilateral Modification Clauses

Perhaps the most insidious: the clause that says the company can **change the terms at any time, and your continued use constitutes acceptance**.

This means the terms you agreed to today might be completely different next month. The company has no obligation to notify you of changes — they just update the page.

Some particularly aggressive versions state that changes are effective immediately upon posting, with no grace period.
